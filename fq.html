Rheumatology FAQs

Find answers to common concerns about our treatments, insurance, and appointments.

What is the difference between osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis?
▾

Osteoarthritis is a degenerative joint disease caused by cartilage breakdown, leading to pain and stiffness. Rheumatoid arthritis is an autoimmune disorder where the immune system attacks joint tissues, causing inflammation and damage.

How long does treatment take?
▾

Treatment duration varies depending on the condition and severity. Some treatments provide immediate relief, while others require ongoing management with medications, therapy, and lifestyle adjustments.

What should I expect during my first visit?
▾

Your first visit will include a detailed medical history review, physical examination, and potentially diagnostic tests such as blood work or imaging. We will create a personalized treatment plan tailored to your condition.

What insurances do you accept?
▾

We accept most major insurance providers, including Medicare and private health plans. Please contact our office for a full list of accepted insurances.
